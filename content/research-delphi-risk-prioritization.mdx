Across three rounds, **272 international experts** ranked which AI threats most
warrant urgent attention, weighing likelihood, magnitude of harm, who is
vulnerable, and who bears responsibility.

### Findings

- The five most concerning near-term harms were dangerous capabilities,
  competitive dynamics, weapons and cyberattacks, power concentration, and
  misinformation.
- Under business-as-usual conditions, eighteen of twenty-four assessed risks
  carried more than a 10% chance of catastrophic outcomes (over one million
  deaths or $100 billion in losses) between 2025 and 2030.
- Experts placed primary responsibility for mitigation on general-purpose AI
  developers and governance institutions.
