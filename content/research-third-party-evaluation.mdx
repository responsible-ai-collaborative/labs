Evaluation conducted by AI developers alone cannot surface the flaws that matter
once systems are deployed at scale. This ICML 2025 position paper — with
contributors from more than two dozen institutions — proposes three
interventions:

1. **Standardized flaw reports** and researcher-engagement guidelines.
2. **Industry-wide disclosure programs**, modeled on cybersecurity bug bounties,
   with legal safe harbors to protect researchers.
3. **Coordination infrastructure** to route vulnerability information to all
   affected parties.

Transferable flaws — jailbreaks that work across multiple providers — show why
disclosure mechanisms comparable to established software-security practice are
overdue.
